Reagents

Bromine Water

Formula: Br₂ (in water)

Answer: Bromine water is used to test for the presence of unsaturation (alkenes). It reacts with double bonds, decolorizing the bromine water, and can also oxidize phenols.

Potassium Permanganate (KMnO₄)

Formula: KMnO₄

Answer: Potassium permanganate is a strong oxidizing agent used in oxidation reactions, including the oxidation of alkenes to diols (Baeyer-Villiger oxidation) and the oxidation of aldehydes to carboxylic acids.

Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH)

Formula: NaOH

Answer: Sodium hydroxide is a strong base used in many reactions, including ester hydrolysis (saponification), aldol condensation, and dehydrohalogenation in the preparation of alkenes.

Sodium Metal (Na)

Formula: Na

Answer: Sodium metal is used in the Wurtz reaction to couple two alkyl halides (R–X) to form an alkane (R–R) in the presence of dry ether.

Aluminum Chloride (AlCl₃)

Formula: AlCl₃

Answer: Aluminum chloride is a Lewis acid catalyst used in reactions such as Friedel–Crafts alkylation and acylation, where it helps to form carbocation intermediates for the reaction to proceed.

Hydrochloric Acid (HCl)

Formula: HCl

Answer: Hydrochloric acid is commonly used in a variety of reactions, including the preparation of alkyl chlorides from alcohols (via substitution) and for the reduction of aldehydes and ketones to alcohols.

Sulfuric Acid (H₂SO₄)

Formula: H₂SO₄

Answer: Sulfuric acid is a strong acid used as a dehydrating agent in reactions like esterification (Fischer esterification) and as a catalyst in reactions like hydration of alkenes.

Sodium Thiosulfate (Na₂S₂O₃)

Formula: Na₂S₂O₃

Answer: Sodium thiosulfate is used as a reducing agent and in the iodometric determination of the concentration of oxidizing agents. It also reacts with iodine to form a colorless solution.

Zinc (Zn)

Formula: Zn

Answer: Zinc is used in various reactions, such as the reduction of carbonyl compounds (Clemmensen reduction) and in the reduction of nitro compounds to amines (Beckmann rearrangement).

Nitric Acid (HNO₃)

Formula: HNO₃

Answer: Nitric acid is used in the nitration of aromatic compounds to form nitro compounds (as in the nitration of benzene to nitrobenzene).

Acetone (CH₃COCH₃)

Formula: CH₃COCH₃

Answer: Acetone is a common solvent and is used in reactions such as the preparation of Grignard reagents and in the reduction of certain carbonyl compounds.

Lithium Aluminium Hydride (LiAlH₄)

Formula: LiAlH₄

Answer: Lithium aluminium hydride is a powerful reducing agent, used in the reduction of esters, carboxylic acids, and other carbonyl compounds to alcohols.

Diethyl Ether (C₂H₅OC₂H₅)

Formula: C₂H₅OC₂H₅

Answer: Diethyl ether is a common solvent used in reactions like the Wurtz reaction and in the preparation of Grignard reagents due to its ability to stabilize reactive intermediates.

Phosphorus Trichloride (PCl₃)

Formula: PCl₃

Answer: Phosphorus trichloride is used for the chlorination of alcohols to form alkyl chlorides and in the conversion of esters to acid chlorides.
